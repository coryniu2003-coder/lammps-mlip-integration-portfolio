The idea without the jargon

Three tools, three different jobs.

01

DFT is the reference

Density functional theory uses quantum mechanics to estimate a material's energy and atomic forces. It is accurate enough to train and test models, but expensive for long simulations.

02

MLIPs are the fast approximation

A machine-learned interatomic potential learns the relationship between atomic structures, energies and forces from reference data. It can then make repeated predictions much faster.

03

LAMMPS moves the atoms

LAMMPS is the simulation engine. It asks the chosen model for forces, updates every atom's position, and writes a trajectory that can be analysed like a scientific experiment.
